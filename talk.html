Endangered Animals

Endangered animals are species that are at high risk of extinction due to a significant decline in their population or the destruction of their critical habitats. This can be caused by a combination of factors such as habitat loss, overhunting, and environmental changes

Reasons for Endangerment:

Habitat Loss: Destruction of natural habitats due to human activities like urbanization, deforestation, and pollution.

Overexploitation: Overhunting, poaching, and trade, particularly in exotic animals, which significantly reduce their numbers.

Causes of species endangerment in the Philippines:

Deforestation and agricultural land clearing: Deforestation results in widespread extinction of both animals and plants as their habitats are destroyed and protective cover is lost. The clearing of forests and meadows for crops like rice significantly reduces forest habitats due to large-scale logging and land conversion for agricultural purposes.

Illegal Hunting and Trade:Poaching and the exotic pet trade are significant threats to wildlife.

Pollution and Climate Change:Pollution, especially in marine ecosystems, and the impacts of climate change affect local species and ecosystems.
